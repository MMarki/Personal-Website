Building a Portfolio Website

Sketching | Code Prototyping | Branding & Visual Design | Implementation

The Problem:

Elissa is an aspiring art director who built her own website using Bootstrap when she was in college. She didn't feel like the website that she had created was a good reflection of who she was as an artist and as a person. She felt it looked too much like a typical out-of-the box Bootstrap website.

Our goal was to rebuild her website from the ground up with a focus on reflecting her personality and effectively showcasing her work. Bootstrap is very customizable, so I decided to leverage Bootstrap and write custom styling that extended the framework beyond its default look.

Sketching:

I drew some sketches to brainstorm how the website should be re-structured to improve information architecture, navigation, and interface design.

I regularly got feedback from Elissa so that we could iterate on these early sketches and come up with something that made sense.

Code Prototyping:

At this point, I jumped right into prototyping the project with code. Elissa wanted to get regular feedback so that she could help mould the site as it took its initial shape. I made the decision to skip over high-fidelity mockups because I knew I would be regularly meeting with Elissa and could save a ton of time this way. I built custom classes to handle navigation and layout, and I re-wrote most of the HTML to accomodate the new architecture of the website.

Branding & Visual Design:

Since this was a personal website, I worked closely with Elissa to establish how the website should look. She wanted the softness and approachability of lower-case letters and pastel purples and blues. When she designed a new logo, I integrated the colors of the logo into the grid of projects. I used one of the purple colors throughout the website for links.

Implementation:

I continued refinind the design of the website with a focus on making it responsive and easy to use.
